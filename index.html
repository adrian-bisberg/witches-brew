An Analysis of: Witches’ Brew: Industrial Scale Data Poisoning Via Gradient Matching

by Adrian Bisberg and Evan Rose

Introduction

Witches’ Brew introduces a novel type of integrity attack wherein a model is trained from scratch using tampered data in order to target a specific image for misclassification. Authors are motivated by creating an integrity attack that improves upon previous work to target larger scale models and work with minimal amounts of poisoned training data. The technique used in their attack is called gradient matching. Here, authors find that the attacker may mimic the gradient of the target by creating poisoned data whose training gradient aligns with the gradient of an adversarial target. The authors define a threat model of an attacker who can control a limited amount of the training data, cannot modify labels of the training data, and has knowledge of model parameters but not entire details of the model architecture.
